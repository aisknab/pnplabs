Significance

A claim at the boundary of efficient computation.

P versus NP is the divide between efficient verification and efficient discovery. The release asserts that the divide can be crossed: SAT is decided in polynomial time through a locked NAND residual-slack construction and an accepted proof-package checker boundary.

If that boundary survives independent review, the consequence is not incremental. It would settle P versus NP in the affirmative and force a re-examination of optimisation, automated reasoning, program verification, proof search, design automation, and assumptions built on computational hardness.
