Public framing and review status.

This page gives reviewers, institutions, and technical readers precise language for discussing the PNP Labs proof-report release without overstating external validation.

What is PNP Labs claiming?

PNP Labs presents a machine-checkable finite-certificate proof-report claim for P = NP over the locked NAND residual-slack package. The public theorem boundary is CheckPCCPackexp(GeneratePCCPack()) = accept ⇒ P = NP.

Is this presented as independently verified by the wider community?

No. PNP Labs presents the accepted proof-report artefact stack and invites independent verification. Community review, institutional assessment, and external formal audit are separate from the release’s internal acceptance record.

Why is the checker boundary so prominent?

The report’s claim is tied to accepted executable artefacts. Reviewers need to know exactly which finite package, acceptance run, replay, final certificate, release gate, and proof-report records are being relied on.

What is the locked NAND role?

The locked NAND construction encodes SAT into an exact-minimization threshold for a tagged multi-output direct-wire word. The report states that these instances have residual slack at most four, allowing the residual-band minimizer to be applied.

What should a technical reviewer inspect first?

For first-pass review, prioritise the claim boundary, locked NAND threshold, residual slack bound, residual-band exact minimization theorem, no-hidden-minimization scan, final package acceptance, and acceptance-replay linkage.

What is the difference between artefact verification and regeneration?

Artefact verification checks the committed sealed bundle and checksum ledger. Regeneration checks that the recorded source/checker revision emits accepted compact and full proof-report records with the expected theorem fields and linkage.

How should media or institutions describe the release?

Recommended public wording: “PNP Labs has released a machine-checkable finite-certificate proof-report claim for P = NP and is inviting independent technical verification.” Avoid language implying external consensus unless that evidence exists.

Where do review requests and security disclosures go?

Technical review, source access, and artefact access requests go to review@pnplabs.com.au. Security issues and suspected artefact-integrity concerns go to security@pnplabs.com.au.
