Architecture

How the claim is organised for review.

The release is organised as a chain of reviewable records: local transformations, residual witnesses, selector realization, ZeroSlack, locked NAND, final replay, and release sealing. This page gives the structure behind the technical package names.

Layer map

Review layers.

Each layer has one basic question: does the previous record justify the next one under the checker discipline? The central invariant is that constructive savings compile to VerifyDWexp, not to quotient equalities, sidecars, or bounds-only evidence.

Direct-wire foundations

Carriers, open functions, compatible supports, replacement, charge ownership, full/quotient projection, and mode firewall.

Local routing and normalization

Traceable normalization, finite tables, critical windows, branch-cycle and unary routers, bounded splices, and ledgers.

Hereditary and budget closure

Hereditary normal forms, budget envelopes, sidecar records, rank-negative blocker edges, and acyclicity.

Residual witness cellization

BCEL-ready positive nuclei, side-tight optima, request envelopes, activation cancellation, shadows, PkgC, and BN6 packets.

Selectors and ZeroSlack

Pair, triple, and spine selectors; typed realizer bots; charge-surplus gains; selector silence; HB closure; ZeroSlack contradiction.

Locked NAND and final seal

SAT threshold construction, residual slack bound, CheckPCCPackexp, final replay, final certificate, release gate, and canonical report.

Trust boundaries

Primary trust boundaries.

Generator boundary

The deterministic generator is not the trusted object. The checker validates the generated package, rows, package references, imports, bounds, and final theorem linkage.

Constructive firewall

Quotient equality, route tokens, sidecar records, and bounds-only evidence cannot stand in for full-mode replacement records.

Release custody

Source/checker origin is separated from the durable artefact-bearing release, with manifest and checksum records available for review.

Reviewer invariant

Every claimed saving must pass the verifier.

Any claimed constructive saving must pass through the direct-wire verifier. A saving that relies only on quotient equality, a route token, a sidecar, or a bounds-only certificate is outside the stated acceptance discipline.
