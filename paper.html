Paper guide

Read the proof report from the checker boundary outward.

The report presents a finite-certificate route through locked NAND exact minimization, residual-band descent, generated package checking, and final acceptance replay. This guide gives a structured order for first-pass technical review.

Claim boundary

The checked theorem boundary.

The public theorem is presented as a conditional accepted-checker statement. The report then records the accepted package/replay/certificate chain that discharges that condition in the sealed artefact stack.

Public theorem

CheckPCCPackexp(GeneratePCCPack()) = accept ⇒ P = NP

The theorem field is tied to the executable acceptance boundary. Review should first establish what accepted, what was replayed, and how the accepted records link to the final statement.

Trust model

Generator untrusted; package checked

The generator emits the package, but the release claim is made at the level of checked artefacts: package checker, acceptance run, replay, final verdict, final certificate, release audit, release gate, and proof report.

Reading order

Suggested order for technical review.

Begin with dependency structure, then move into line-by-line verification.

Executive status and claim boundary

Confirm the theorem record, antecedent, consequent, conditional flag, and checker trust model.

Direct-wire framework

Track carriers, open functions, compatible supports, replacement, charge ownership, and the mode firewall.

Residual-band minimization

Follow NormalizeOrGain, HResolve, BudgetResolve, selector realization, HB closure, ZeroSlack, and PCCMin.

Locked NAND SAT embedding

Check the threshold construction, macro distinctness, trace equivalence, final-lock separation, and residual slack bound.

CheckPack and package manifest

Audit row keys, proof references, import graph, no-hidden-minimization scanning, reflection, and polynomial bounds.

Final release seal

Inspect accepted report fields, digest ledger, sealed release identifiers, and the two independent verification modes.

Core concepts

Technical terms used throughout the report.

TermMeaningReview relevance
Residual slackΛ(C) = |C| − μ(C), the gap between a circuit and its minimum equivalent size.The descent measure is residual slack, not root circuit size.
Locked NANDA tagged multi-output direct-wire construction used to encode SAT into an exact-minimization threshold.The SAT decision depends on the threshold gap and residual slack bound.
PCCPackThe generated proof-carrying package checked by CheckPCCPackexp.Package acceptance is the antecedent of the public theorem boundary.
ZeroSlackThe no-gain branch that derives zero residual slack from selector silence and HN/BUD closure.It is the bridge from exhaustive negative evidence to exact minimization.
Mode firewallThe discipline preventing quotient-mode equality from being consumed as a constructive full-mode replacement.It protects the soundness of every claimed saving.
No-hidden minimizationA scan rejecting executable uses of hidden minimization or canonical optimality symbols.It supports the claim that the checker remains explicit and polynomial.
