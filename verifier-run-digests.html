Historical semantics

What the digest comparison did

The old utility normalised record fields and compared SHA-256 digests for historical verdict data, activated-status coordinates, checker-script outputs, logs, and complete records. A digest match means the normalised historical bytes agree. It does not verify the truth of an assertion carried by those bytes.

Identity only

Hashes and comparison cells identify matching historical record content. They do not prove theorem correctness.

Superseded boundary

The records contain old activated fields. The authoritative current status has theorem establishment and emission set to false.
